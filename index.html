Global collaboration for an ultrarare condition

The FCS International Project unites countries to build a harmonised dataset and shared research outputs, strengthening evidence for Familial Chylomicronemia Syndrome (FCS).

Learn about FCS Collaborate / Contact

What we do

Harmonise international data and enable shared analyses across participating sites.

Why it matters

Ultrarare diseases require pooled cohorts to build meaningful evidence and trial readiness.

Get involved

Researchers, clinicians, and collaborators are welcome—use the Contact page to reach us.

Project aim

To unite partners worldwide to develop a shared evidence base for FCS while improving awareness, facilitating diagnoses, and supporting future research activities.
